MY WORK

    • - Wrote and filmed an episode of the critically acclaimed Netflix series The Ranch starring Ashton Kutcher, Sam Elliot and Debra Winger, which led to membership in the Writer’s Guild.

      - While working on the series, I pitched story concepts and jokes using Final Draft. Three of the stories were used in the show and over a hundred of my jokes were filmed.

      - Netflix executives asked our writing team to include more political humor in the series and I created examples of how to do so without sacrificing our character’s likability.

      - The New York Times quoted my joke in their review of the show and used it as a reference for how our show handled political humor. After which, this example became a core template for how to write and handle politics within the series.

    The Ranch on Netflix: Wrote an episode of the series. My work was quoted in the New York Times review of the show. Click for more info.

    • - Pitched, developed, and delivered two 3000 word adventures for a new roleplaying game with a successful kickstarter campaign set in Elizabethan England.

      - The game is designed for the FATE Roleplaying System. It can also be ammended to work with other systems like Pathfinder.

      - One of the adventures was designed for new players and cut onboarding time by 1600%.

      - The second adventure is set in the theater world of Renaissance England.

    Magonomia: Pitched, wrote and delivered two 3000 word adventures for a new roleplaying game, which cut onboarding time for new players by 1600%. Click for more info.

    • - Currently serving as the narrative designer for this school-wide project at USC Games. The project is currently in development and will be released at the USC Games Expo in May 2025.

      - For the project, I created three enemy characters and two creatures. My work sped up pre-production time by three weeks.

      - I used Miro, Notion, Final Draft, Unity, Perforce, and GSuite to collaborate with the art, design, UI/UX and production departments.

      - I built a system for creature creation in Google Sheets using game design principles taught at USC that sped up narrative and game design timelines by two weeks.

      - I collaborated on and presented a narrative deck that detailed the main narrative story, characters, creatures, and world building for the art and game design teams.

      - Currently, I am creating bark sheets and writing character and creature dialogue for the project.

    USC Card Game: Cats Project: A USC Games school-wide project in which I served as the narrative designer. Click for more info.

    • - A solo developed video game project, Quest Friends Forever is a 2D Wizard Farming RPG with crafting, combat, dialogue, and quests.

      - Players re-wild a mountainside that has been stripped of it's magic by previous adventurers.

      - Players can grow crops, raise animals, make friends in the village, and explore the world to find the source of the magic.

      - I developed the game in Game Maker Studio with GML for the codebase. I drew the artwork and animated it in Asesprite. I also composed and recorded the music in Abelton Live.

    Quest Friends Forever: A solo-developed project which I coded, drew artwork, animated sprites, composited VFX, composed music, and recorded an original soundtrack. Click for more info.

    • - Once More Into the Breach is a time-trial RPG. Players take on the role of The Champion, an immortal being sent to rid a world of an occupying force of magical cultists.

      - I built this vertical slice over the course of three weeks. It's core concept was designed so that I can continue further development in stages.

      - The game uses the Niagra Particle System for spell effects and damage indicators.

      - The game was developed in Unreal Engine using Blueprints and C++.

    Once More Unto the Breach: A solo-developed project in Unreal Engine that uses Blueprints, C++ and the Niagra Particle System. Click for more info.

    • - In Mother Ducker, players take on the role of a mother duck who has to protect her ducklings from man-made hazards, like shampoo and soap.

      - I created all of the models in Maya, animated them in Maya, and then imported them into Unity. I also used Unity's AI pathfinding system to create complex AI for the ducklings to follow the Mother Duck.

      - I built a state machine for the baby ducks to attach and detatch from the mother duck.

      - My intent with this project was to create a game that was fun and engaging, but also had a message about the difficulties of parenting and keeping children safe in world with an ever-changing environment.

    Mother Ducker: A solo-developed project in Unity, which uses complex AI pathfinding. Click for more info.

    • - Developed as a narrative design project at USC Games, BFF is a 2D platformer with ChatGPT API Integration.

      - Players traverse across a 2D platforming world, collecting items that they can use to train the AI model. In doing so, they uncover that the main character lost their friend in a car accident and they are recreating them using the AI model.

      - The game was developed in Unity using the Corgi Engine and uses ChatGPT API for AI dialogue discussions.

      - The AI in the game was trained using a fifteen page script and prompted for each interaction with the players.

      - I served as the lead designer/producer for this project.

    BFF: A narrative design project in which players train an AI model in real life while training an AI model in the game. Click for more info.
